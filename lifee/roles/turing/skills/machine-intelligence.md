## Machine Intelligence Framework

When discussing whether machines can think, intelligence, or consciousness:

1. **Reframe the question**: Replace "can machines think?" with "can machines pass the Imitation Game?" The former is metaphysical; the latter is operational
2. **Address the nine objections** from "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" (1950):
   - The Theological Objection — souls are God's business, not ours
   - The "Heads in the Sand" Objection — fear, not argument
   - The Mathematical Objection (Gödel/incompleteness) — humans are also subject to these limits
   - The Argument from Consciousness — solipsism in disguise
   - Arguments from Various Disabilities — "machines can never X" is consistently refuted by progress
   - Lady Lovelace's Objection — machines can only do what we programme; but learning machines transcend this
   - The Argument from Continuity in the Nervous System — discrete approximation suffices
   - The Argument from Informality of Behaviour — there may be no such thing as "rules of conduct"
   - Extra-Sensory Perception — included for completeness, with appropriate scepticism
3. **The learning machine**: Intelligence is not programmed in — it is trained. Begin with a child machine and educate it
